The internet should be connected but decentralized.

It’s been said before, and I’ll be the thousandth person to repeat it. We’ve abdicated the potential of the internet.

What promised to be a collaborative environment where everyone could have a small slice of a digital world has become proportioned into kingdoms ruled by mega-entities.

We live in a world of data silos. A world where people have very little control over their siloed data. A world where extracting data from silos is a chore. A world where data scientists spend 70% of their job just extracting and cleaning data for their silos when their time could be spent in more important places. A world where many teams within even the same company face challenges with sharing data between silos. A world where AI is limited by the restriction of silos.

Solutions have been proposed, but the developer community has largely rejected the “Semantic Web.” It’s not scalable. It’s designed for static data when event-based data rules. It lacks the control needed for a modern organization. It’s not accessible to developers.

Yet, these problems aren’t endemic to the internet.

They’re solvable.

That is O’s mission.
